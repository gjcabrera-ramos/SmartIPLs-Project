Smartphone Physics Labs

Innovation • Accessibility • Education

Transforming undergraduate physics education through smartphone-based laboratories.

Explore Research

Introduction

Smartphones have emerged as accessible and scalable tools for experimental physics education. Their integrated sensors allow the implementation of laboratory experiences in low-resource contexts while promoting educational equity.

Objectives & Research Questions

Main Objective

Analyze the pedagogical and technical conditions necessary to implement smartphone-based physics laboratories in low-resource environments.


Research Questions

  • How feasible are low-end smartphones?
  • Which methodologies fit Ecuadorian contexts?
  • Can SmartIPLs replace traditional laboratories?
  • What technical limitations affect devices?

State of Art

Limited Infrastructure

Limited laboratory access constrains STEM learning.

Technical Feasibility

Smartphone sensors provide measurements comparable to standard instruments.

Pedagogical Impact

Improves engagement, autonomy and higher-order thinking skills.

Implementation

Teacher training and institutional support remain essential.

Discussion

The effectiveness of smartphone laboratories depends on technical capabilities, pedagogical implementation and institutional support.

Conclusions

  • SmartIPLs are technically viable.
  • Support active learning.
  • Reduce infrastructure barriers.
  • Require standardized protocols.
